Teaching Machines to Think Like Humans

The Tsinghua Knowledge Engineering Group (KEG) is dedicated to advancing the frontiers of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) and empowering global academic discovery through AMiner.org.

Our Three Pillars

Teaching machines to think like humans, at scale, and for the world.

AGI Vision

Pursuing artificial general intelligence by teaching machines to think, reason, and understand the world in human-like ways.

Scalable Intelligence

Building large-scale foundation models and systems that turn AGI research into real, deployable intelligence.

Open Impact

Advancing open science through shared models, platforms, and global communities, creating broad scientific and societal impact.

Global Impact & Adoption

We conduct long-term research on large-scale foundation models for language, vision, code, and agent, and have developed a family of representative models including GLM, ChatGLM, GLM-V, CogVideo & CogView, CodeGeeX, and AutoGLM. These models are widely adopted by both academia and industry worldwide. More models are available on our GitHub. We also built AMiner.org, a global academic knowledge platform serving researchers across disciplines.

Recent Research

GLM-5: From Vibe Coding to Agentic Engineering

GLM-5 targets complex systems engineering and long-horizon agentic tasks. It scales from 355B (32B active) to 744B (40B active) parameters, increases pre-training data to 28.5T tokens, and integrates DeepSeek Sparse Attention (DSA) to reduce deployment cost while preserving long-context capacity.

Recent Summit

AGI-Next Summit

The AGI-Next Summit is an invitation-only forum initiated by Tsinghua University, focusing on the key pathways of next-generation artificial general intelligence and the role of open-source ecosystems in China. The summit brings together leading researchers and industry practitioners to discuss emerging paradigms, agentic systems, and future challenges in global AGI development.

Award

SIGKDD Test-of-Time Award

Our ArnetMiner paper received the ACM SIGKDD Test-of-Time Award for Applied Science, recognizing its long-term impact since 2008. The award honors the ArnetMiner system, a pioneering platform for extracting and mining large-scale academic social networks.
